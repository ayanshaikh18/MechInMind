WHY 5 ARTICLES DAILY? - THE CHUNK CONCEPT


Learn why we upload just about 5 articles daily and how it makes you smarter every day.

by Aishwarya Chauhan


Learning by chunking is an active learning strategy characterized by chunking, which is defined as cognitive processing that re-codes information into meaningful groups, called chunks, to increase learning efficiency or capacity. Chunks of information are generally composed of familiar or meaningful sets of information that are recalled together. In this way we are able to decrease the amount of information that must be held in working memory by increasing the amount of information per chunk.

Chunking is a strategy used to reduce the cognitive load on the brain as the learner processes information. Essentially, chunking helps in the learning process by breaking long strings of information into bite sized chunks that are easier to remember.

The process involves several steps. The number of steps you use depends on the type of information you are trying to remember.

  1. Break larger amounts of info into smaller units
  2. Identify similarities or patterns
  3. Organize the info
  4. Group info into manageable units
The following examples will emphasize the importance to use chunks:
  1. Phone numbers can be remembered as 860-552-562 rather than 860552562.
  2. A long name of something can be remembered as X-LET-HIPS-OX-WHY-MIX rather than XLETHIPSOXWHYMIX.
  3. Long lists can also be chunked:
    • List: bread, ice cream, milk, tomatoes, eggs, butter, apples, muffins, vegetables, bagels, lettuce, cream, banana.

      Chunked list:
      Fruits and vegetables: lettuce, banana, tomatoes, apples, and other vegetables
      Bakery: muffins, bread, bagels
      Dairy: milk, eggs, butter, cream
      Frozen: ice cream
